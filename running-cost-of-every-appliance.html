Most energy advice has you fretting over the wrong end of this table.

What the table actually tells you

Three lessons fall straight out of sorting by yearly cost. First, the things that make heat dominate: showers, dryers, immersion heaters, panel heaters and the rest sit at the top, because turning electricity into heat is expensive and there is no efficient way around it. Second, a few modest-looking devices cost more than you would think simply because they never switch off, like a fridge-freezer, an aquarium or a set-top box left on around the clock. Third, the gadgets people are told to fret about, the phone charger and the standby light, are at the very bottom, costing about a pound a year each.

The practical takeaway is the one the whole site is built on: chase the top of the table, ignore the bottom. Unplugging your phone charger to save 1 pound a year while a tumble dryer quietly costs over 120 pounds is effort spent in exactly the wrong place. Put your own appliances through the running cost calculator to get your real numbers, and if the bill itself looks wrong, work through why your electricity bill is so high.

For the big users specifically: the heating guides cover electric heat and hot water, and Economy 7 and night rates can cut the cost of anything you are able to run overnight.
